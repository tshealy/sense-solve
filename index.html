Built Environment Design Game

Sense & Solve

Draw a built environment problem, a sensing method, and an optional constraint. Then develop a realistic solution that turns data into a decision and a real-world action.

Your Challenge

Use the generated cards to design a practical system that addresses the problem using the assigned sensing method. The goal is not just to collect data, but to show how that data becomes useful for a decision-maker.

What your team should produce

  • What decision is being improved
  • What data is being collected and how it is gathered
  • How the data is processed or interpreted
  • What output the system produces, such as a map, alert, ranking, or dashboard
  • Who uses the output and what action they take
  • The biggest limitation or weakness of your approach
Be realistic Your idea should make sense in the real world, not just in theory.
Use the assigned sensor You may not replace the sensing method with a different one.
Acknowledge limitations Good design includes what might fail, bias, or break down.

Suggested class use: 3–4 students per team, 15–20 minutes to develop a concept, then a 2-minute pitch.

Prompt Starters
